🛡️ Makerspace

Makerspace Safety Rules

The Makerspace is an exciting place — and keeping it safe is everyone's responsibility!

🔧

Power Tools

  • All power tools are completely OFF-LIMITS
  • Robots use "no-cut" building methods
  • Heavy machinery is taped off at all times
  • Handheld power tools locked away
🧹

Cleanliness

  • Pick up after yourself always
  • Wash hands regularly throughout the day
  • Remove all food from tables after meals
  • Return materials to their place
🏃

Conduct in the Space

  • No running inside the Makerspace
  • No tag, hide & seek, or active games inside
  • Active games are outside during rec time
  • Regular headcounts keep everyone safe
🍎

Food Policy

  • No snacks inside the Makerspace
  • Snacks eaten outside during snack break
  • Lunch CAN be eaten inside
  • Clean up food messes immediately
📋 Code of Conduct

Camper Behavior Contract

Every camper agrees to these expectations when registering. Please review with your child before camp! 💙

💙

Our culture: We build people UP, not tear them down. Every camper should feel safe, included, and excited to learn!

✅ Required Camper Behaviors

  • No weapons of any kind
  • No alcohol or drugs
  • Wear appropriate, non-offensive clothing
  • No sexual activity or harassment of any kind
  • Be safe — do not put yourself, others, or property in harm's way
  • Follow all instructions from volunteers and counselors
  • Be kind to campers, staff, property, and yourself
  • Build people up — never tear them down
  • Follow the published schedule — be ready to start and stop on time

⚠️ Consequences for Not Following Rules

1
First Response
Temporary removal from activity and separation from the group.
2
Sent Home for the Day
Parents notified and must arrange pickup. School administrators notified.
3
Suspended — No Refund
Suspended from the remainder of camp with no refund. Police may be contacted for illegal acts.
🏥 Health & Safety

Medical & Health Policy

Please read this carefully before camp. Key information for parents!

🚨

No nurse on campus during Robocamp. Staff cannot administer medication. If your child needs medicine during the day, YOU must come to campus to give it.

💊

Medication Policy

  • Staff will NOT give any medication
  • No exceptions outside true emergencies
  • Parents come to campus to administer it
  • Includes inhalers, EpiPens, daily meds
🌿

Allergies & Medical

  • Disclose all allergies in the registration form
  • Disclose any medical disabilities or conditions
  • Camp staff use this info to take precautions
  • Contact us if your child's needs change
🚑

Emergency Treatment

  • Parents sign emergency medical consent in waiver
  • On-site supervising teacher can authorize treatment in emergencies
  • Parents notified immediately in any medical situation
  • Call 911 first in life-threatening emergencies

📄 Legal & Consent

Waiver & Consent

🚨

Required: All campers must have a signed waiver to participate. No waiver = no camp.

📄 The Waiver Covers:
  • Emergency medical treatment consent
  • Acknowledgement of risks of physical activity
  • Full release of liability for injuries or damages
  • Media consent — photos may be published by Westwood Robotics
  • Parent or legal guardian signature required
ℹ️

The waiver is included in the registration process at westwoodrobots.org/robocamp. Questions? Email westwood_robotics@roundrockisd.org.
